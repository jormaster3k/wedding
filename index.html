#easternbirdwedding

Eastern Bird? What's that all about?

One of the biggest questions when getting married is what to do about last names. Traditionally (in many cultures at least), the bride takes the groom's last name as her own. We decided pretty quickly that this was not for us. We also thought about double-barreled last names (e.g. Osterman-Ptaszek or Ptaszek-Osterman) but decided against that as well. What if we just kept our current last names? This is the easiest option by far (fewer trips to the DMV), but we decided it was important to us to share the same last name.

Then we found out that in New York State, it's possible to create an entirely new surname without difficult legal proceedings, so long as it contains parts of either of the newlyweds surnames. We thought that this was a wonderful way to ensure that we will continue to view ourselves as equals in marriage, just as we have in our relationship (read: do our part to smash the patriarchy). Plus, we thought it was just cool.

So we took what we consider the most "meaningful" part of our last names, Osterman (Man from the East) and Ptaszek (Little Bird), and merged them together to form a new surname for our future... Osterptak ... or, Eastern Bird!
